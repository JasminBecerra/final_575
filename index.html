Chicago Public High Schools: College Readiness

The state of Chicago Public Schools (CPS) has garnered attention in discussions on inequality in American public education. In early 2017, Mayor Rahm Emanuel proposed a new set of graduation requirements for CPS high school seniors. In particular, one additional requirement for high school seniors has resulted in backlash from the public:

proof of acceptance into college or the military, or a trade or a ‘gap-year’ program

So, what does college readiness look like in Chicago?

Overview

NETWORKS

Chicago Public Schools organizes its district-run schools into 13 networks. Networks are defined as geographic regions in the city. CPS does not group Charter, Contract, or SAFE /ALOP schools (“schools managed by independent operators for students who have been expelled from other schools due to violence” and “provide educational options for students who have dropped out of school and seek to return” respectively) into any of the 13 geographic networks.

Out of 172 high schools, 95 were district run for the 2016-2017 school year.

Because we are looking strictly at CPS’s 13 geographic networks, the data visualized in our map and elsewhere is restricted to only the schools that are categorized into a geographic network.
